# ISA Tax Context

**This page is a reference, not advice.** Tax rules change; check
[gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/individual-savings-accounts) or a regulated
adviser for the rules that apply to you today.

## The ISA wrapper in one paragraph

An Individual Savings Account (ISA) is a UK tax wrapper. Investments held
inside it are shielded from **dividend tax** and **capital gains tax (CGT)**
for as long as the wrapper stays open. You do not declare ISA income or
gains on a self-assessment return.

## The annual allowance

Each UK tax year (6 April → 5 April) you can contribute up to a statutory
allowance across all your ISAs. As of the 2025/26 tax year this is **£20,000**
— verify on [gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/individual-savings-accounts) as the
number can change. The allowance does not carry over: unused allowance is
lost at midnight on 5 April.

## Types relevant to this book

- **Stocks & Shares ISA** — holds cash, funds, ETFs and individual shares.
  This is what the book's portfolio lives in.
- **Cash ISA** — interest-only; out of scope here.
- **Lifetime ISA (LISA)** — separate £4,000 annual allowance with a 25%
  government bonus but penalties on non-housing/non-retirement withdrawals.
  Complements but doesn't replace a Stocks & Shares ISA.

## Flexible vs non-flexible

A **flexible** ISA lets you withdraw and replace money within the same tax
year without losing allowance. A **non-flexible** ISA does not. InvestEngine
is non-flexible; Trading212 ISAs are flexible at the time of writing —
confirm with the provider.

## Bed & ISA

Moving existing holdings from a taxable General Investment Account into an
ISA usually requires selling (realising any CGT) and repurchasing inside the
wrapper. Some platforms offer a "Bed & ISA" service that batches the
sell/buy. It still counts against the annual allowance and may trigger CGT
on the sell leg.

## What ISAs do NOT protect you from

- **Stamp duty** on UK share purchases (most ETFs are stamp-duty-exempt
  anyway).
- **Withholding tax** on foreign dividends (e.g. the 15% US withholding on
  dividends from US-domiciled ETFs — another reason this book sticks to
  UCITS ETFs domiciled in Ireland, which get treaty relief).
- **FX risk** on unhedged funds holding foreign assets.
- **Market risk** — the whole point of investing.

## What the book ignores

All returns reported in the notebooks are computed **net of fund [TER](99_glossary.md#costs) only**.
The figures do not account for platform fees (InvestEngine and Trading212
are currently free for ETF trading), dealing spreads, or tax on any cash
held outside the wrapper. In an ISA, tax on the wrapped assets is nil, so
gross-of-tax and net-of-tax returns are identical.
